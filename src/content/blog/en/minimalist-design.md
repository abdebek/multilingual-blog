Minimalism is more than just an aesthetic choice; it's a philosophy that prioritizes content and function over unnecessary decoration.

## The Core Principles of Minimalism

### 1. Less is More
Every element on the screen should serve a purpose. If it doesn't contribute to the user's goal, remove it.

### 2. Negative Space
Whitespace (or negative space) gives your content room to breathe and helps users focus on what's important.

### 3. Visual Hierarchy
Use typography and scale to guide the user's eye through the content naturally.

By embracing minimalist design, you create products that are not only beautiful but also intuitive and easy to use.
